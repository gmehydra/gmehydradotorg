Why Fediverse?

There are pros and cons with any model of social media.

Centralized

We are mostly familiar with centralized social media models such as Reddit, Twitter, YouTube, Facebook, etc.
Centralized models work for most people most of the time. But, crucially, centralized social media models mean that ultimate authority is always in the centralized control of the platform owners and never with the community. This can naturally lead to certain kinds of issues, the most notable being censorship.

Decentralized

Due to this fundamental flaw with centralized models, various attempts have been made to create a solution that could be described as decentralized, with one of the main ideas being that more decentralization creates more resistance to censorship. The definition of the word decentralized can be ambiguous but in this context it would mean a network of software nodes interacting with similar such nodes that operate without any human intervention at all. Bitcoin and Ethereum are two examples of networks that meet this description. While some attempts have been made at decentralized social media, it is a challenging problem for such products to be functionally decentralized while also being a tool that is effortlessly useable and appealing.

Federated

Somewhere in between centralized and decentralized exists federated. A federation is a group of computing or network providers agreeing upon standards of operation in a collective fashion. In a federated network, there are aspects that are centralized and aspects that are decentralized. While a federated network is not a fully decentralized solution, it is a mostly decentralized solution.

The Fediverse

While ideally the ultimate censorship-resistant solution would be a properly decentralized one, that ideal decentralized solution does not exist right now.
The fediverse exists right now.

The fediverse is comprised of tens of thousands of nodes and somewhere between 3 and 10 million active users.

In the fediverse, Mastodon is the software project that is being by the largest number of users and has the most nodes.
