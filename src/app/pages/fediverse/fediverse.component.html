Why Fediverse?

There are pros and cons with any model of social media.

Social Media Models
Centralized

Centralized social media models such as Reddit, Twitter, YouTube, Facebook, Twitch, etc., are the most common and familiar types of social media on the Internet.

In this context, the defining aspect of centralized models is that there is a single central authority at the top of the structure with ultimate control over the entire network.

Centralized models work for most people most of the time, but, because ultimate authority is always in the centralized control of the platform owners and never with the community, this imbalance of power can create circumstances where opposing interests between the community participants and the community owners results in censorship.

Decentralized

Due to this fundamental weakness with centralized models, various attempts have been made to create a solution that could be described as decentralized, with one of the main ideas being that more decentralization creates more resistance to censorship.

The definition of the word decentralized can be ambiguous but in this context it would mean a network of software nodes (aka servers) interacting with similar such nodes that operate autonomously without any human intervention at all. This is the definition that is typically used in the context of cryptocurrency.

Bitcoin and Ethereum are two examples of networks that meet this description, but these networks are cryptocurrency networks and not social media networks.

While some attempts have been made at decentralized social media, it is a challenging problem for such products to be functionally decentralized while also being a tool that is effortlessly useable and appealing.

Federated

Somewhere in between centralized and decentralized exists federated.

A federation is a group of computing or network providers agreeing upon standards of operation in a collective fashion.

In a federated network, there are aspects that are centralized and aspects that are decentralized. While a federated network is not a fully decentralized social media solution, it is a mostly decentralized social media solution.

The Fediverse

While the ideal censorship-resistant solution would be a properly decentralized one, that ideal decentralized solution does not exist right now.

The fediverse exists right now.

The fediverse is a federated social network that is composed of many different software projects.

The fediverse runs on tens of thousands of nodes and has a population of between 3 and 10 million active users.

Among fediverse software projects, Mastodon is the software project that is being used by the largest number of users and has the largest number of running nodes.
